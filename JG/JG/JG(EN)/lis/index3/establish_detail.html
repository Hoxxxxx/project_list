Steps of Establishing Alumni Association

If you are interested in setting up an alumni association, please refer to the following steps:

Who is eligible to join and form an alumni association?

1. Graduates, undergraduates, advanced students, exchange students, international exchange students, and short-term exchange students of CUHK-Shenzhen;

2. Those who have taught and served at CUHK-Shenzhen;

3. Those who have received CUHK-Shenzhen's honorary degrees or titles;

Note: Alumni without a degree are allowed to participate in any alumni association or its events, but are not allowed to form a new alumni association.

How should an alumni organization be set up?

The following steps are applicable to undergraduate and postgraduate graduates.

Step 1: Establish the preparatory committee

To establish a school-approved alumni association, a preparatory committee with 5 members at least is required. These members should be considered as initiators and may later serve in key positions within the preparatory committee, such as the Chairman, financial staff and secretaries.

Step 2: Notify the Alumni Affairs and submit the application form

Inform the Alumni Affairs of establishing an alumni association and submit the application form. The Alumni Affairs group will:

1. Confirm that there are no overlapping alumni organizations and estimate the number of members of the school/branch according to the alumni database.

2. Contact other alumni with similar intention so as to help set up the preparatory committee.

3 .Schedule a meeting to discuss issues related to the alumni association.

Step 3: Attend the meeting

The preparatory committee members participate in the first meeting to discuss and determine the following issues:

1. The purpose of the alumni association and its future development goals.

2. Organizational structure, responsibilities and the list of recommended staff.

3. Membership and estimated number of members.

4. Finance and asset management.

5. Proposal (containing the cosignature of 100 alumni) and alumni association charter.

Step 4: Submit the proposal, application, articles of association/school registration

In order to establish a school-approved alumni association, a petition containing at least 100 alumni names and their cosignature is needed. Alumni are required to provide personal information such as their majors, schools, graduation time and contact details. Alumni in favor of the proposal need to clearly support the mission of the alumni affairs group of CUHK-Shenzhen and the alumni association and undertake to join this organization. Note: The alumni information list is exclusively used by the staff of Alumni Affairs of CUHK-Shenzhen and members of the committee.

After the proposal, application and draft articles of association are submitted, the alumni affairs group should review them and submit to the school's vice president (external and student affairs) for approval. A notice should be issued as soon as possible after the school has made a decision.

Step 5: Register the social organization at the local Civil Affairs Bureau

Contact the local Education Bureau and register at the Civil Affairs Bureau. The registration process is as follows:

1. Approval of the name of the alumni association.

2. Alumni association registration. The preparatory committee should fill in the application form and submit relevant documents online as required.For the list of documents, please refer to the website of social groups registered with the local civil affairs bureau. http://www.szmz.sz.gov.cn/cn/ztfw_yw/qsxshtt/cbdj

Step 7: Alumni association register

The Alumni Affairs should maintain a list of the approved and registered alumni association roll and articles of association.Any alumni association registered in the roll, if there is any change in its organization and articles, should be submitted to the Alumni Affairs for updates. The dissolution of alumni association, if any, should also be reported to the Alumni Affairs and cancelled from the roll.
